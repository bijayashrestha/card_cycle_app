01

The Float Window

Every card has a statement close date and a due date (21–25 days later). Spend right after the statement closes — your purchase won't appear until the next statement, giving you up to ~55 days interest-free.

02

The 4-Card Rotation

Space your 4 cards' close dates roughly 7–10 days apart. This creates a rolling cycle where there's always one card in its "fresh" window. Each week, rotate to the card that just closed its statement.

03

Never Pay Interest

Always pay the full statement balance on or before each due date — never just the minimum. The float strategy only works if you avoid interest charges, which would cancel all the benefits.

04

Automate Payments

Set each card to autopay the full statement balance from your bank account 3 days before the due date. This protects your credit score and ensures you never miss a payment.

05

Track & Adjust

Use the Calendar tab to see all close and due dates at a glance. If two cards have close dates too close together, call the bank to request a statement date change — most banks allow this once every 6 months.
